## Post 1 — Thu Aug 27: The problem

Built Complyra in 90 days. Turns out, EU AI Act compliance for SMEs is completely broken.

Here's what I mean: if you're a 10-person SaaS company with a customer-facing chatbot, you are — right now — subject to a piece of EU regulation with real deadlines (August 2, 2026 for starters). Your options are basically:

1. Hire a law firm you can't really afford
2. Hope nobody notices
3. ??? (this didn't exist before)

Spent the last few months talking to founders and compliance people about this. More on what I learned in the next post.

## Post 2 — Fri Aug 28: Validation + learnings

Here's what I learned interviewing companies about their AI compliance process:

- Almost nobody had a real inventory of the AI systems they build, buy, or use
- Of the ones who'd heard of the EU AI Act, most didn't know which of the four risk tiers they fell into
- Nobody had a repeatable process for re-checking a system after they'd changed it

The common thread: this isn't a "we don't care" problem, it's a "we don't have the tooling" problem. Compliance work for a 10-person team looks completely different from a 500-person team's legal department, and nothing was built for the small end.
