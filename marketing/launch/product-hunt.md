# Product Hunt Launch — Complyra

**Target date:** September 1, 2026 (early morning, ~06:00 AM PST)
**Maker:** Asım

## Tagline

Automate your EU AI Act compliance in hours, not months.

## Description (2–3 paragraphs)

Complyra automates EU AI Act compliance for SaaS companies and SMEs. Answer a short set of guided questions about the AI systems you build, buy, or use, and Complyra classifies each one against the Act's risk tiers, explains the reasoning in plain language, and generates the technical documentation, conformity records, and transparency notices your risk tier requires.

We built this because EU AI Act compliance for small teams is currently either "hire a law firm" or "hope for the best." Neither works for a 10-person startup with a chatbot and a hiring tool. Complyra gives you a defensible, documented compliance posture without a legal team.

Article 50 transparency obligations (chatbot/AI-content disclosure) go live August 2, 2026 — if that's the first time you're hearing that date, Complyra's free risk scanner will tell you in under two minutes whether it applies to you.

## Maker comment (post at launch)

Hi Product Hunt! 👋

We built Complyra because EU AI Act compliance for SMEs is broken. If you're not a 500-person company with an in-house legal team, your options right now are basically "pay a law firm a lot of money" or "hope nobody asks."

We talked to compliance folks and founders at SaaS companies before building this, and the pattern was the same everywhere: nobody had actually inventoried their AI systems, nobody knew which risk tier they fell into, and the August 2026 transparency deadline was creeping up on people who didn't know it existed.

Complyra automates the whole pipeline: inventory → risk classification → document generation → ongoing tracking as deadlines approach. It's not legal advice — it's a documentation preparation tool that gets you 90% of the way there and tells you clearly where you still need a professional.

Free tier is genuinely useful (not just a trial), and the risk scanner takes about 2 minutes with no signup. Would love your feedback — especially if you're outside the EU and unsure whether this even applies to you (spoiler: it might, see /export-compliance).
