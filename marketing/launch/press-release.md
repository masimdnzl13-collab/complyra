## Complyra Launches AI-Powered EU AI Act Compliance Platform for SMEs

**[City], September 1, 2026** — Complyra, a self-serve compliance platform for the EU AI Act, launched today with fewer than two weeks left before the regulation's first major deadline. Article 50 transparency obligations — requiring AI systems that interact with people or generate synthetic content to disclose that fact — take effect August 2, 2026, and Complyra's founders say most small and mid-sized companies aren't ready.

Complyra automates the core compliance workflow for teams that can't justify a dedicated legal or compliance hire: inventorying AI systems, classifying them against the Act's four risk tiers, and generating the technical documentation, conformity records, and disclosure notices each tier requires. Classification combines deterministic legal rules for clear-cut cases with AI-assisted review — powered by Anthropic's Claude — for genuinely ambiguous ones, and borderline cases can be routed to a network of compliance consultants for paid expert review.

"The existing options for a ten-person SaaS company were a law firm you can't afford or hoping nobody asks," said Asım, founder of Complyra. "[Additional founder quote on validation/traction to be finalized closer to launch.]"

Complyra offers a free tier and paid plans starting at €49/month, billed in EUR through LemonSqueezy as merchant of record. The platform is built on Next.js, Firebase, and Vercel, and is available now at complyra.io.

### About Complyra

Complyra helps teams generate and manage the documentation required for EU AI Act compliance — risk classification, technical documentation, and conformity records — without the manual paperwork. Complyra is a documentation preparation tool, not a law firm, and does not provide legal advice.
